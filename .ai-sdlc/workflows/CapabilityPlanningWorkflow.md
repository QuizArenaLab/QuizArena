# Capability Planning Workflow

## Governing Authority
All actions are strictly governed by the AI SDLC Constitution:
[.ai-sdlc/constitution/AI-SDLC-v1.2.md](../constitution/AI-SDLC-v1.2.md)

## Objective
To ensure that all execution planning prioritizes the delivery of complete business capabilities rather than isolated technical features.

## Capability Definition
A Capability is a complete business outcome that can be demonstrated to an end user. Capabilities are the smallest unit of production-ready delivery.

## Capability Lifecycle
Capabilities follow this lifecycle:
`DISCOVERED` -> `PLANNED` -> `VERIFICATION PLANNED` -> `APPROVED` -> `IMPLEMENTING` -> `UNDER REVIEW` -> `PASSED` -> `LOCKED` -> `RELEASED`

## Launch Priorities
Launch planning shall prioritize capabilities using:
- **P0:** Launch Critical
- **P1:** Launch Essential
- **P2:** Launch Enhancement
- **P3:** Future Version

Capability priority takes precedence over technical convenience.

## Sequence Example
A delivery model for a launch may follow this sequence:
1. Sprint 01: Competition Management
2. Sprint 02: Assessment Experience
3. Sprint 03: Competition Registration & Commerce
4. Sprint 04: Results & Rewards
5. Sprint 05: Product Experience
6. Sprint 06: Production Readiness

Each capability must be production-ready before the next capability sprint begins.
